Amazon Case Study:

Increasing sustainability awareness and purchase transparency
through redesign of existing features.

There has been a shift within industries towards shifting to more eco-friendly products + services,
as well as increasing societal pressure to change and heightened demand for products marked as sustainable.
NYU Stern Center for Sustainable Business conducted a study using IRI provided data, which consisted of
73,293 products purchased across 36 categories, and they were able to determine that consumer packaged products
marked as sustainable experienced significant market growth.
Image Source: https://www.stern.nyu.edu/sites/default/files/assets/documents/NYU%20Stern%20CSB%20Sustainable%20Market%20Share%20Index%202020.pdf

We wanted to see how much people knew about sustainability, what their interest in it was,
if they used mobile applications to make sustainable purchases, and what mobile applications they used.

With over 150 million users, Amazon is currently the most popular mobile shopping application in America.
However, many users are unaware of Amazon's pledge “to be net-zero carbon by 2040”,
and their partnerships with multiple institutions to offer certified products that promote sustainability.
